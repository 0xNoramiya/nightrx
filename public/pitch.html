The Problem

Getting medication shouldn't
require exposing your diagnosis

Stigma

HIV, mental health, addiction patients avoid treatment because picking up medication reveals their condition

Data Exposure

Prescriptions pass through pharmacists, insurance systems, databases — each a potential leak point

Real Harm

In many communities, a disclosed HIV diagnosis leads to discrimination, job loss, and social exclusion

The Insight

A pharmacy doesn't need to know why
you need medication

They only need to know: "This person is authorized to receive medication X, verified by a trusted clinic."

No diagnosis. No identity. Just proof of eligibility.

Why Midnight

Built for privacy-first applications

ZK Proofs (Halo 2)

Prove statements without revealing data. Sub-second verification, no trusted setup.

Selective Disclosure

Patients choose exactly what to share. Only the proof result is visible — never the underlying data.

Compact Language

TypeScript-like smart contracts with built-in privacy primitives. Witnesses keep secrets off-chain.

This system cannot be built on Ethereum, Solana, or any transparent chain. Midnight's privacy model is essential.

Smart Contract

Three circuits, real ZK proofs

registerIssuer Clinic registers as trusted credential issuer. Proves identity via private key witness.
issueCredential Stores credential commitment on-chain. Only the hash is public — no medical data exposed.
verifyPickup Patient proves eligibility via ZK proof. Contract checks commitment exists, nullifier unused.

Privacy Model

What the pharmacy never sees

On-Chain (Public)

  • ✓ Credential commitment (hash)
  • ✓ Nullifier (unlinkable hash)
  • ✓ Issuer registry
  • ✓ Dispensation count

Never On-Chain

  • ✗ Patient identity
  • ✗ Diagnosis or condition
  • ✗ Full credential details
  • ✗ Which medication is for which condition
